Spatial 3D Note-taking in XR

Transforming flat notes into embodied spatial memories for designers, students, and researchers.

View Case Study

Project Overview

Role

UX Designer, Researcher

Duration

12 weeks

Tools

Figma, Unity, Blender, Miro

Team

Solo project with mentorship

Problem Statement

Students, designers, and researchers are stuck taking notes on flat, 2D screens — this limits spatial memory formation, reduces creativity, and makes organizing complex, interlinked ideas difficult.

Big Idea

An XR note-taking environment that turns thoughts into placeable 3D objects, letting users organize knowledge spatially using natural hand gestures and body movement.

Value Proposition

Spatial 3D Note-taking in XR is the only personal note system that leverages 3D placement and embodied interaction to improve recall, insight generation, and creative exploration — so users can think and remember in three dimensions.

Key Features

Natural Hand Gesture Input

Intuitive gestures for creating, moving, and organizing notes in 3D space

Spatial Organization & Clustering

Group related ideas naturally in virtual space with visual connections

Memory Anchors

Create memorable spatial locations that enhance recall and understanding

Cross-Context Export

Seamlessly export spatial arrangements to traditional formats

Who / What / Why

Who

Students, researchers, and creative professionals who work with complex, interconnected ideas

What

Need a way to externalize complex ideas beyond linear lists and flat documents

Why

Human cognition naturally uses spatial memory to understand and recall information

Key Insights

Current note-taking apps force linear, 2D organization that doesn't match how we think. Ideas are interconnected webs, not sequential lists. Complex problems require seeing relationships between concepts, but screen space limitations prevent natural spatial arrangement. Spatial memory is fundamental to human cognition and recall, yet our digital tools ignore this completely.

Design Challenge

How might we enable embodied spatial note-taking for students and creatives so that they can think, organize, and recall complex ideas more effectively?

Solution Focus

Help users remember and recombine ideas by giving them spatial anchors and embodied interactions.
